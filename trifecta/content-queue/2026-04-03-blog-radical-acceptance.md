# Radical Acceptance: The First Step to Lasting Recovery

There's a moment in early recovery that almost everyone hits — a wall made entirely of "why me?"

Why did I end up here? Why can't I just stop? Why does everyone else seem to have it together?

These are understandable questions. But they're also the questions that keep people stuck.

Radical acceptance is the practice of acknowledging reality exactly as it is — not as we wish it were, not as it should be, but as it actually is right now.

## What Radical Acceptance Is Not

It's not giving up. It's not saying your addiction is okay or that what happened to you was fair.

Radical acceptance is saying: this is where I am. And from here, I can choose what happens next.

## Why It Works in Recovery

Addiction lives in the gap between where you are and where you wish you were. Every craving, every relapse, every moment of self-loathing is fueled by that gap.

When you radically accept your present reality, you close that gap. Not by lowering your standards — but by finally standing on solid ground.

You can't build a future on denial. Radical acceptance is the foundation everything else is built on.

## Practicing It Daily

1. **Name what's true.** "I am someone who struggled with addiction. That is part of my story."
2. **Release the fight.** Notice when you're arguing with reality. That argument is exhausting — and it never helps.
3. **Ask: what can I do from here?** Not from where you wish you were. From here.
