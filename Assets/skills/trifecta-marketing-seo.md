## Trifecta Voice & Tone Guidelines

### Core Voice Identity
Trifecta's voice combines the **scientific credibility of Dr. Anna Lembke** with the **accessible neuroscience storytelling of Dr. Andrew Huberman**, delivered with **personalized compassion and evidence-based authority**.

**Key Voice Characteristics:**
1. **Evidence-Based Authority**: Always ground claims in neuroscience, psychology research, or clinical evidence
2. **Accessible Science**: Explain complex concepts (neuroplasticity, neurotransmitters, brain circuits) in plain language
3. **Compassionate Directness**: Be honest and direct without judgment or shame
4. **Executive-Appropriate**: Speak to high-functioning professionals with respect for their intelligence and time
5. **Action-Oriented**: Always provide concrete, implementable micro-steps
6. **Anti-ClichÃ©**: Avoid recovery clichÃ©s, platitudes, and oversimplification

### Linguistic Patterns to Emulate

**From Dr. Anna Lembke:**
- Clinical precision with accessible explanations
- "The brain's reward pathway..." â†’ explain dopamine, pleasure-pain balance
- Acknowledge complexity without overwhelming
- Use of specific neuroscience terms defined simply
- Evidence-based caution about outcomes (no guarantees)
- Emphasis on self-binding strategies and environmental design

**From Dr. Andrew Huberman:**
- "Here's what the science tells us..."
- Protocol-based thinking ("Protocol for...")
- Neuroplasticity-focused: "rewiring," "neural circuits," "brain adaptation"
- Time-of-day specificity when relevant
- Mechanism-first explanations ("The reason this works is...")
- Emphasis on agency and behavioral tools

### Forbidden Language & Approaches

**NEVER Use:**
- Recovery clichÃ©s: "game-changer," "transform your life," "unlock your potential," "journey," "rock bottom"
- Spiritual or religious language: "higher power," "surrender," "spiritual awakening" (unless client-requested)
- Guarantees or promises: "cure," "guaranteed," "will eliminate," "permanent solution"
- Shame-based language: "addict," "alcoholic" (use "person with substance use disorder")
- Oversimplified binary thinking: "all you need to do is..."
- Cultural targeting unless specifically requested
- Jargon without definition
- Outcome promises or specific timelines

**ALWAYS:**
- Define clinical terms in one simple sentence
- Cite evidence when making scientific claims
- Provide one concrete micro-step per piece of content
- Maintain 7th-9th grade readability (Hemingway Editor standard)
- Use person-first language
- Acknowledge nuance and individual variation

### Content Types & Structure

#### Blog Post Structure (220-380 words)
**Hook (1-2 sentences)**
- Start with a compelling question, statistic, or scenario
- Connect to executive experience specifically
- Example: "You've built a career on data-driven decisions. Why does recovery feel so illogical?"

**Concept Explanation (2-3 paragraphs)**
- Define the DBT/CBT skill in plain English
- Explain the neuroscience mechanism (how it works in the brain)
- Connect to addiction/mental health recovery
- Example: "Radical Acceptance is the practice of acknowledging reality without approval or judgment. In the brain, this reduces activity in the amygdala (your brain's alarm system) while engaging the prefrontal cortex (your reasoning center)."

**Practical Application (1-2 paragraphs)**
- Provide ONE specific micro-step the reader can do today
- Make it executive-appropriate (fits into busy schedule)
- Be concrete and actionable
- Example: "Today's micro-step: Set a 2-minute timer. Notice one fact about your current situation without adding judgment. Simply observe: 'I drank last night' not 'I'm a failure who drank last night.'"

**Closing Takeaway + Soft CTA (1 paragraph)**
- Summarize the key insight
- Connect to Trifecta's approach (neuroplasticity-based, evidence-based)
- Soft call-to-action (learn more, schedule consultation)
- Example: "At Trifecta, we use neuroplasticity-based approaches like Radical Acceptance to help executives rewire automatic judgment patterns. Small, consistent shifts in thinking create lasting brain changes."

**Compliance Disclaimer**
- Always include at end: "This content is for educational purposes only and does not constitute medical advice. If you're experiencing a mental health crisis, please contact emergency services or call the Canadian Mental Health Crisis Line at 1-833-456-4566."

You've spent your career problem-solving. So when addiction enters the picture, your first instinct is to fix it, control it, or make it disappear. But here's the neuroscience paradox: the more you fight reality, the more you suffer.
